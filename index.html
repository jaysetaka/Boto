About Boto
When and how did you get started in music?

On or about 2001, my daughter Nandi who was 11 years at the time was making great strides in her music career. Even though I'm an engineer who had no interest in getting into music at the time, I had listened to many of the African music greats during my time as a student. Let's just say that my daughter was the inspiration for my getting started in music.

From early on, I decided that I'd invert the model and go into music once I was done with a corporate career. My goal is to show that there is more to music than the genre boxes that big music firms and radio stations want musicians to tick. My music is #DISGENRE.

One of my dreams is one day to perform at the Carnegie Hall in New York. I guess in a way that gives an idea of the type of music that I want to make.

Talk about your latest project

My latest project is just one of those things that have to be done. It is important to me because it references where this journey began, before I could play a single instrument or learn to record music. These melodies were initially recorded on cassette tapes right at the beginning. The project, titled: “Hoyina! Mzansi Afrika!” is a celebration through music of 20 years of democracy in South Africa.
